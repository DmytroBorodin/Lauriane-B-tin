Evolution of Montblanc boutique concept

2019 - 2020

Montblanc Gmbh. Retail Design and Store Planning

Montblanc is a German luxury company which produces luxury goods such as writing instruments, watches and leather goods. I was being part of the International Retail Design Department which focused on store planning. My work in general was to rethink the boutique concept and create a new experience into the stores by designing new furniture or updating former ones. In six months, I developed the first Newsletter of the department and mainly worked on three creative developments : Personalization, SLG MLG Presentation and Gift Corner, from the design to the supervision of the production drawings furnished by our suppliers.

Montblanc Boutiques

Three years ago, Noé Duchaufour-Lawrance created Neo², a brand new concept for Montblanc boutiques. The concept has evolved ever since and tends to always be brighter and dynamic. A boutique is composed of wall units and free-standing units, and each unit displays one kind of products. My work was to develop the design of different modules in order to implement them in the future boutique opening. I always had to refer to Isabelle Cappuccio, the director of Retail Design and Store Planning department.

Brief.

Update the wall unit module dedicated to personalization.

  • Make it clear that it is a personalization area
  • Display small leather goods, tags, wrist handles, stickers
  • Showcase different font types of the embossing machine

My goals as an interior designer

  • To understand people’s behaviors in a boutique
  • To understand store planners’ needs and take their insights into consideration
  • To share my ideas in a clear way to different stakeholders (Visual Merchandising, Retail Merchandising and Category Management)
  • To offer a different point of view on the projects thanks to my design process
  • To iterate on design and bounce back if the project was not approved
  • To supervise production drawings of Montblanc suppliers

Personalization.

My design process for Montblanc will be demonstrated through the Personalization project. The Personalization has for purpose to allow customers to personalize their leather goods. First of all, we set up a meeting with all the stakeholders to define needs for everyone. Then, we gathered every week minimum to discuss the project evolution.

Picture of the former Personalization module.

01. How do I start a project?

When I create a new design, my first stage is to study the current module and to see its weaknesses and strengths, how the products are displayed and how it could be improved. After this study, I look for inspiration and at the trends in magazines, websites and places that I visited. I gather all the ideas that I find interesting and coherent and create a moodboard with key words to explain my ideas to my director.

02. How do I conceptualize?

I start with some quick sketches to explain to the stakeholders what I aim to do. After approval, I modelize on a 3D software (SketchUp) and launch some renders (with Vray). I explore different ideas and I present them for approval. I explain the pros and cons of each one, depending on the budget and how easy it is to implement it into a boutique.

With Isabelle Cappuccio and one of my former colleague Silvia Lucca, we decided to create steps which will address directly to the customer and will make him understand what to do. We also placed the tools and products that will be used to conceive their personalized leather good, such as coloured foils, different fonts stamps and customized leather goods examples. To help stakeholders understand how the Personalization unit will look like, I integrated (via Photoshop) the module in a picture of a real shop. With this integration, we can see the size and products in the surrounded units.

What happens if I don’t get the approval?

I iterate on designs and explore new ideas again. I talk to my colleagues to have their inputs, re-focus on the moodboard, find other inspiration sources and conceptualize again until I get the approval.

03. How to develop the approved idea?

After approval of all stakeholders, I create the layout (AutoCAD) of the module and write down the quotations and maximum details (materials, lighting, assemblage). It serves me to point out all the technical aspects of the unit and list every component. We can also print out the technical drawing details at scale 1 to know how the customer will see it, if it is the right size, if the products are easy to reach. I allows us to understand client’s behavior and bring modifications at the drawings if needed.

How to be sure that everything will be aligned in the boutique?

We have a special room for prototypes at scale 1, sent by our suppliers. I used to go there to measure all the modules next to the one that I was working on.

04. How to ensure production?

Our suppliers Oldenburger are responsible for the production of Montblanc store furniture. I was the one assuring the liaise with them. I was sending them renders and technical drawing details of the project. They studied them and proposed me production drawings. I had to review them, see if something was missing, add some notes and send them back the drawings. This cycle could last until the production drawings were perfect.

Why do we need a prototype?

After the production drawings validation, we usually ordered a prototype to place in the prototypes room. It allowed us to see how it looks like in reality before rolling it out worldwide.

Finalization of the project

After the prototype approval, the production is launched

  • The Personalization unit will be announced in our Newsletter
  • The current boutiques will be implemented by the new module
  • Store planners will integrate them in their new projects
