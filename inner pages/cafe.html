Merchant Suites Café

2017

DAAA Haus. Commercial design

DAAA Haus has been a successful design agency for more than ten years. Headed by Keith Pillow, this maltese company won different awards and is known for its luxurious commercial projects, especially its restaurants and boutique hotels. It was my first internship abroad and my first experience working in English. It was definitely a great one, in an amazing team of creative and passionate people. During my time at DAAA Haus, I helped interior designers on various projects and on different tasks. It made me understand their project methodology and taught me different management tools. At the end of my internship, they gave me for responsibility to conceptualize Merchant Suites Café which was located on the ground floor of Merchant Suites Hotel in Valletta.

Brief.

Keith Pillow showed me one inspiration picture so I had to create a similar design

  • Find other inspiration pictures
  • Work on the layout to fit a certain number of seats
  • Design bespoke furniture for bar, shelving and booth
  • Create technical drawing details for production
  • Develop the bill of quantities

My goals as an interior designer

  • To conceive an identity similar as the hotel and the inspiration picture
  • To make construction workers understand my drawings
  • To find furniture brands that will have a short delivery period (as Malta being an island, deliveries take always longer than other countries)
  • To have a better idea on furniture costs and production costs

Merchant Suites Café.

Valletta, maltese capital, is classified as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Therefore, it is almost impossible to touch any wall of any building. My work for Merchant Suites Café had one more challenge and I had to design the project with the actual building shape.

01. How did I start this project?

I searched for other inspiration pictures that looked similar to the one I already had and according to the current hotel design.

02. How do I conceptualize?

As I could not remove any wall, I had to design bespoke furniture so they can fit properly in the space. Working on the layout at the same time was necessary and I also had to learn all sizes regulations for the furniture.

Pictures of the existing space.

Pictures of the existing space.

03. Development of technical drawing details

All details are very important: materials, lighting, shape and how it is assembled. Construction workers have to understand quickly or the construction site will be delayed.

The shelving was the most difficult furniture to explain as it contains a hidden door and has a back and a front structure. I used colors so the workers will understand which part is before and which one is behind.

04. Bill of quantities

It was the first time I created this type of listing. I used the base of DAAA Haus’ bill of quantites and listed all the components needed for the café. I also conceived a design booklet with references and pictures of listed elements in the bill of quantities.

Finalization of the project

The furniture production and construction site started while I was working on the design.

  • I visited the construction site with DAAA Haus.
  • We checked together how the site was progressing and took pictures if something was wrong.
